# Kalendar — Independent Product Review
**Date:** 2026-07-18
*(First review — no prior baseline to compare against.)*

## 1. Bottom line

The core booking loop — clinic signs up, sets up business/services/team/hours, and a patient books a real slot with no double-booking — genuinely works end-to-end, and the code is clean, consistently conventioned, and well-documented for its stage. But the product is not yet a business: **there is no payment collection anywhere in the codebase** despite it being a headline pitch item, and **there are no appointment reminders** despite that being the other headline pitch item and the primary no-show-reduction mechanism. Right now Kalendar is a well-built scheduling page, not yet the "cut no-shows and collect payment" product it's sold as. The single biggest risk to €1M ARR is sequencing: the team's own backlog has reminders ranked #1, which is right, but there's no monetization mechanism at all yet — no plan gating, no Stripe, not even a stubbed "coming soon." Every day spent on secondary polish before either of these two ships is a day the product can't be sold on its stated value prop.

## 3. What's genuinely strong

- **The booking wizard and slot engine are solid.** Double-booking is prevented at the DB level via a partial unique index (`kalendar_bookings_active_slot_idx`), not just app-layer checks — this is the right place to enforce it and will hold up under concurrent requests.
- **Auth/security layering is disciplined.** Every mutation is independently authenticated via `authedAction`, not just gated by the panel layout — this is exactly the right posture given there's no RLS backstop (service-role key everywhere).
- **i18n architecture is unusually mature for this stage** — cookie-based, dictionary-per-module, guest locale persisted per booking row. Most solo-founder SaaS products don't bother with this until much later, and retrofitting it is painful; doing it now is a good bet if Spain-only isn't the permanent plan.
- **Guest booking flow (auth-optional) is a real conversion-rate decision, done well** — forcing account creation before a first booking is a classic drop-off point for solo practitioners' clients, and the guest path with a 24h pending-confirmation window is a sensible middle ground.
- **The admin portal's schema-reset tooling (confirm-phrase gate, preflight, audit log)** is more careful than most pre-revenue products bother with — this will pay off when live clinic data is on the line.

## 4. Gaps that block revenue or retention (ranked)

1. **No payment collection mechanism at all** — no Stripe, no deposit capture, no plan/tier table, nothing that bills anyone. *Why it matters:* the product's own pitch and business goal both assume revenue collection is part of what you're selling ("payment collection" to clinics, and a subscription to reach €1M ARR) — right now neither exists, so there is no revenue mechanics story to tell a single design partner, let alone scale to €1M.
2. **No reminder emails (24h/1h) despite being the flagship no-show-reduction feature** — only transactional confirm/cancel/under-review emails exist; "Get automatic reminders" is landing-page copy with no backing implementation. *Why it matters:* this is the core value proposition on the tin. Selling it before it exists is a trust risk with early clinics, and it's the single highest-leverage thing to ship next since the email infra and `starts_at` already exist — this is genuinely close.
3. **No plan/tier enforcement, so there's currently nothing to gate a "premium" anything behind** — the roadmap already lists multi-login as a premium-tier feature, but there's no mechanism anywhere to have tiers in the first place. *Why it matters:* every future monetization idea (multi-login, deposits, advanced reporting) is blocked on this same missing primitive; building it once, generically, now avoids re-doing it three times.
4. **Owner has zero visibility into failure** — if a reminder bounces or a booking silently fails, there's no owner-facing signal. *Why it matters:* a clinic that misses a no-show because a reminder silently failed to send will blame the product, not the transient Resend failure, and won't renew.

## 5. Blind spots not currently tracked

- **No analytics/reporting for the owner at all** — no booking volume, no no-show rate, no revenue-implied numbers. A clinic owner evaluating whether this tool is "working" has no evidence beyond checking the calendar manually. This is usually one of the first things that drives a €X/month subscription to feel justified.
- **Health-adjacent data handling (GDPR)** — the buyer list explicitly includes psychology and physiotherapy clinics. Patient names, appointment types, and booking history for a psychologist's clients is sensitive personal data under GDPR (Spain/EU), and there's no visible data-processing agreement, retention policy, or even a privacy policy page found in the codebase. This is a sales blocker with more sophisticated clinics, not just a nice-to-have.
- **Mobile experience for the owner is untested in this review** — panel is presumably responsive given Tailwind, but a solo practitioner checking bookings between clients is very likely on a phone, and this wasn't verified as a first-class flow (vs. the public booking page, which clearly was designed mobile-first).
- **Nothing blocks or discourages a clinic from just... not finishing setup.** There's a checklist, but no nudge (email, in-panel banner escalation) if a clinic signs up and stalls at "Negocio" for a week. Time-to-value is short *if* the owner pushes through — but there's no mechanism pulling them back if they don't.
- **Word-of-mouth/referral loop is entirely absent** — no referral code, no "powered by Kalendar" branding on the public booking page for organic discovery among other clinics in the same building/professional network, which matters a lot in a tight geographic market like Madrid clinics.
- **No waitlist for fully-booked slots** — a secondary no-show/retention lever (fill cancellations automatically) that's common in this category (Fresha, Booksy) and isn't tracked anywhere in the backlog.

## 6. Technical risk with business consequences

- **Single point of failure: Resend email is "best-effort" (logs and skips on failure) everywhere, including the confirmation and reminder path once built.** For a business whose core value prop is reducing no-shows via reminders, a silently-skipped reminder is a silent product failure with real financial consequences for the clinic. This needs at minimum an owner-visible failure signal before reminders ship, or the flagship feature will fail invisibly in production.
- **Cron sweep is hourly via GitHub Actions, not real-time.** A `pending_confirmation` guest booking can sit unconfirmed for up to an hour past its intended expiry before the sweep catches it — fine at low volume, but this is exactly the kind of manual/coarse mechanism that needs revisiting before scaling past a handful of clinics per the review's own risk criteria.
- **No retry/idempotency visible around `sendEmail` calls in the booking action paths** — a transient Resend outage during a spike (e.g. a clinic sends a bulk migration of bookings) could silently drop a batch of confirmation emails with no record.
- **The onboarding leftover shared-types issue flagged in `MODULES.md` itself** (`DayId`/`BusinessType` living in a folder for a feature that no longer exists, imported by half the app) is a low-urgency but compounding tech-debt item — it will make the next payment/plan-tier schema change harder to reason about if left much longer, since it blurs which module actually owns core booking types.

## 7. Recommended next 3

1. **Ship appointment reminders (24h + 1h before) — this is already #1 on the backlog and it's correct.** It's the closest gap to closing (infra exists), it's the literal headline value prop, and every week it's not live is a week the product is being sold on a feature it doesn't have.
2. **Stand up minimal payment infrastructure — even just a Stripe subscription checkout gating panel access after a trial period, no deposits/no in-booking payment yet.** This wasn't previously tracked as urgent, but it needs to be: there's currently no path from "clinic uses the product" to "clinic pays for the product," which is a harder gap to close than it looks once other features assume a certain schema shape. Build the plan/tier primitive now, generically, since multi-login and future premium features already assume it'll exist.
3. **Add an owner-facing failure/health signal for emails (reminder didn't send, slot double-booking attempt blocked, etc.) before or alongside shipping reminders.** This is cheap relative to the trust cost of a silent failure in a feature literally called "reminders."
